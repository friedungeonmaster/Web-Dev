Travel to Japan

Japan is the first country to greet the dawn. But the main feature of the land of the rising sun are its inhabitants. Who else on the planet admires cherry blossoms so much, being able to build trains rushing at the speed of light?

Perhaps it is the fact that the Japanese are among the first to wake up on the planet that affects their way of life. Hardworking, sun-caressed, benevolent people immediately have to themselves, and their reverence for the traditions of their ancestors, diligence and incredible desire for development causessincere respect.

Japan is part of the Pacific Ring of Fire, which is the cause of many natural disasters, which, nevertheless, could not break the people of Japan. The country is invariably associated with reliability, quality and clarity all over the world.

The best time to visit Japan

Spring and autumn are considered the best time to visit Japan. In spring, cherry blossoms apricot trees, and then everything else begin to bloom, and beautiful warm weather sets in.Summer - humidity, monsoon rains, the possibility of typhoons. In late July - early August, hot, humid weather sets in. Autumn is characterized by dry warm weather, the difference in daytime and nighttime temperatures, and is an attractive time of year to visit the country. At this time, tours to Japan "Admiring maples - momiji" are popular, because there is no such colorful autumn as in Japan.Winter is below zero in the north of the country, but there is no severe cold, in the south it is always warm, there is no snow, in the central part there is mild pleasant weather.

Cities and resorts of Japan

Tokyo is the largest metropolis, leading to amazement and dumb delight of guests. The combination of the traditions of the past centuries with an ultra-modern culture, contrast in all spheres of life, and every piece of this city of the future deserves attention, from ancient Buddhist temples to the Tokyo railway station.

Odaiba Island is an artificial island connected to Tokyo by a Rainbow Bridge with an autopilot monorail, where many shopping malls are located. Here it is worth visiting Onsen Oedo Monogatari - a luxurious spa complex and a theme park on hot springs. This is a great place for the first acquaintance with Japanese onsen baths, and just a good and useful entertainment for all ages.

Kyoto is the second most popular city in Japan, it is called the heart and soul of the country. It is surrounded by mountains on three sides, In Kyoto you can feel the spirit of the medieval era, the aroma of the past is hovering here - Buddhist temples, palaces, tea houses, Japanese cuisine. It is a pleasure to be in Kyoto during the cherry blossom, ajisai or blushing autumn momiji. As for food, the whole range of Japanese cuisine is presented in Kyoto, ranging from gourmet kaiseki dinners to ramen noodles. In addition, there are many Italian, French and Chinese restaurants in the city. Japanese restaurants are usually located in traditional wooden houses with cozy gardens.

Every year, Kyoto hosts colorful festivals Gion Matsuri(July 1-31), Aoi (May 15 – geranium Festival), Gozan (August 16 – festival of lights) and Jidai (October 22), which attract Japanese from other regions of the country, as well as tourists from all over the world.

Tips for tourists in Japan:

  • Tipping is not accepted, full payment is welcome;

  • Handshakes are not accepted, it is necessary to make small bows for greeting, as it is done by local residents;

  • When communicating with people, be restrained, do not gesticulate, follow the rules of etiquette, and remember - the Japanese always smile, even if the situation is not entirely pleasant. It is also not recommended to look directly into the eyes;

  • Smoking is allowed only in strictly designated areas;

  • Chopsticks should not be waved, pointed and stuck into food vertically;

  • In order to show the owner of the house that you like the dish (soups), you need to smack your lips;

  • When you sit on the tatami, you need to take off your shoes, you also need to change into slippers when entering the house;

  • Tattoo holders are prohibited from entering most swimming pools, baths, baths;

  • Traffic on the road is left-handed;
